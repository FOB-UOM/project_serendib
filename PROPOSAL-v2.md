### Executive Summary

Sri Lanka is at a critical crossroads.

The **Digital Education Transformation Policy** (Cabinet submission March 2026) and the **National Education Policy Framework** demand practical, localized tools for teachers. At the same time, **NDC 3.0 (2026–2035)** and the **Clean Sri Lanka Initiative** require accessible, Sinhala-first knowledge on climate adaptation and sustainability.

Both national priorities share one missing foundation: **high-quality, open, sovereign Sinhala data** that reflects real classroom realities and environmental contexts.

**Project Serendib v2** fills this gap by creating a clean, sustainable, multi-type open data ecosystem:

- **Pillar 1**: Teacher Empowerment Toolkit  
- **Pillar 2**: Environment & Sustainability Knowledge Base  
- **Shared Layer**: Advanced Sinhala OCR Improvement Pipeline  

This is a **data-first, zero-budget, long-term faculty seed project** designed to run for years with minimal maintenance — a true Digital Public Good that any AI (local or global) can use.

### Project Architecture

**Pillar 1 – Teacher Empowerment Toolkit**  
Real daily resources for Sri Lankan teachers:  
- Curriculum-aligned lesson plans (Sinhala & English)  
- Differentiated worksheets and low-resource activities  
- Rubrics, assessment tools, and remediation cards  
- Parent communication templates  
- Local-context concept explanations  

**Pillar 2 – Environment & Sustainability Knowledge Base**  
Actionable, localized data aligned with national climate goals:  
- Climate adaptation guides and policy summaries  
- Biodiversity and green practices corpus  
- Sustainable agriculture and waste management materials  
- School-level environmental education resources  

**Shared Layer – Sinhala OCR Improvement Pipeline** (Signature Contribution)  
We will systematically improve Sinhala OCR (especially handwritten and messy documents) using Tesseract + Qwen2-VL hybrid + SinOCR dataset. This layer becomes a reusable national utility that feeds both pillars and future projects.

---

### Core Principles

- Data-only (no model training or inference required for years)  
- 100% open-source, sovereign, zero ongoing cost  
- Two clean, independent pillars (no forced mixing)  
- Student-powered curation via open-source Argilla  
- Platform-agnostic design (GitHub core + simple web door)  
- Built to survive even if the founder steps away  

---

### Engagement Model

A simple, inclusive web interface (minimal Streamlit + Argilla) allows:  
- School children → easy OCR correction tasks  
- Teachers → verify or contribute lesson plans  
- Citizens → sustainability contributions  

No GitHub knowledge required. Data flows automatically into the open repository.

### Phased, Sustainable Roadmap

**Phase 0 (Q2–Q3 2026)**: Repository + OCR baseline + first samples  
**Phase 1 (2026–2027)**: Teacher Empowerment MVP  
**Phase 2 (2027–2028)**: Environment & Sustainability pillar + advanced OCR  
**Phase 3 (Ongoing)**: Community growth as a living faculty project  

Designed so that one motivated student per year can keep it alive indefinitely.
